Steak

Description

Steak is a piece of meat cut from cattle. It originated from a variety of countries: Italy, Scandinavia and England. It contains lots of nutritional value, and is associated as a "super-food". Steak is usually made as tender, juicy and rich in flavour and cooked over a grill; there are many different ways to cook it depending on your preferences. Finally, it is often seasoned with many different spices to enhance its flavour.

Ingredients

  • Steak
  • Butter

  • Seasonings
    • Garlic
    • Cilantro
    • Thyme
    • Parsley
    • Salt
    • Pepper

Steps

  1. First bring the steak to room temperature, this makes the searing more even.
  2. Next pat the steak dry and generously season it with salt and pepper. This helps form that amazing crust we all know and love about great steaks!
    3. Now bring out your pan and get it SMOKING HOT before putting the steak in. Then add the butter first and run it across the pan.
  3. Next, add the thyme but be cautious the butter will sputter when you add it!
    4. Now take the steak off the stove BEFORE your desired internal temperature. Because the internal temperature will continue to rise as it rests.
  4. Finally, rest your steak for 5 to 10 minutes so it sucks its own juices back in and the fibres relax. This is a must-do step for any protein you cook hard and fast!
