Spaghetti

Description

Spaghetti a dish originating from Italy can be described as white cylindrical noodles. It is made from wheat and water. Many people pair it with their own custom sauce. Sauces usually consists of tomatoes with dashes of garlic, parsley and other seasonings to add to its flavour. This dish is usually served as a main course, so remember to pair it with cheese and a glass of wine!

Ingredients

  • Spaghetti
  • Ground Meat
  • Tomatoes and Tomato Paste

  • Seasonings
    • Garlic
    • Onion
    • Spices

Steps

  1. Start by browning your meat in a bit of oil, then stir in onions until they soften. Then add lots of flavor with garlic, tomato paste, oregano, and red pepper flakes. Finally add a bit of water or broth and crushed tomatoes! Simmer the sauce for about 25 minutes, about 15 minutes before start cooking your spaghetti.
  2. Cook your spaghetti in salted water. Add one tablespoon of salt for every four quarts of water. Spaghetti takes 8 to 12 minutes. Set a timer for 8 minutes then check your spaghetti to see if it needs longer. You can tell its ready when the insides of a strand is no longer white.
  3. Do not rinse your spaghetti, there is no need to rinse it after cooking. Especially if you plan to toss it with a sauce. Rinsing will remove the starch, which helps the sauce cling to the noodles.
  4. Finally, remove the spaghetti with a strainer, and separate it into different portions. Start scooping the sauce onto the spaghetti and shred parmesan cheese on top of it. Voila Enjoy!
